# The 8 Laws of Change — as our operating spine

**Adrian-direct:** apply Schwartz's *8 Laws of Change* across **all** ventures as an overarching principle. This file is the cross-venture doctrine.

## What this is (and the honest caveat)
Schwartz derived the eight laws as the **traits every successful nonviolent change movement shares** — not as a business framework. So this is an **adaptation**: the 8 Laws become our **ethical + operating SPINE — the *why* and the *how*** — they do **not** replace commercial rigour (pricing, funnels, the accountant, delegation-first). They **direct** it. Where a law maps natively to a venture I say so; where it's a translation from "movement" to "business," I flag it. Used honestly, they're a near-perfect fit for the consciousness ventures (Ashta, SS, AGA) and a genuine integrity-check on the commercial ones (OSB, Tri Hita, Bodhisvara).

## The reconciliation that makes it usable (the one objection, answered)
"A profit-seeking venture with *no cherished outcomes* and *no need for credit* is a paradox." It isn't:
- **Hold the intention firmly (Law 1); hold the specific outcome loosely (Law 2).** You are committed to the mission; you are non-attached to *this* sale / *this* launch landing exactly as imagined.
- **Non-attachment ≠ indifference to success.** It is **bias-reduction + resilience** — and that is *literally Schwartz's own research finding*: a cherished outcome introduces observer bias and distorts judgement. Holding outcomes loosely makes you *see more clearly and adapt faster*. (It's the same ethic as "publish the null" and "no attachment to which side of the argument wins" — Adrian's own stance.)
- So the 8 Laws don't soften the venture; they remove the desperation and self-deception that wreck ventures.

## The eight laws (verbatim) + what each means for us
1. **"The individuals, individually, and the group, collectively, must share a common intention."** → Every venture + every team member works to one clearly-articulated, life-affirming intention. The intention is the fixed point.
2. **"The individuals and the group may have goals, but they may not have cherished outcomes."** → Pursue goals hard; stay non-attached to the exact result. Test, let the data decide, drop what doesn't work without ego. (Bias-reduction, not indifference.)
3. **"The individuals must accept that their goals may not be reached in their lifetimes and be okay with this."** → Build on a long horizon; plant trees whose shade you may not sit in (AGA, Tri Hita, Ashta's dataset, the regenerative cooperative). Patience as strategy.
4. **"…may not get either credit or acknowledgment for what they have done and be authentically okay with this."** → Do the right thing whether or not it's seen or credited; don't optimise for applause. (Frees genuinely better decisions.)
5. **"Each person… regardless of gender, religion, race, or culture, must enjoy fundamental equality, even as the various roles in the hierarchy of the effort are respected."** → Fundamental dignity + fair dealing for everyone — clients, suppliers, artisans, members, staff — even within respected roles. (The cooperative/equality thread.)
6. **"…must forswear violence in word, act, or thought."** → Translate to business: **no coercion, no manipulation, no false scarcity, no fear-selling, no bad-faith tactics** — in copy, negotiation, or internal culture. Persuasion stays honest.
7. **"…must make their private selves consistent with their public postures."** → **Radical congruence: the public claim must match the private truth.** No fabricated testimonials, no overclaim, no hidden agenda. (This *is* the firewall doctrine, generalised.)
8. **"…must always act from the beingness of life-affirming integrity."** → The ground of being: every venture should, on net, **affirm life** — and integrity is non-negotiable. The final filter on any decision.

## How it threads with existing doctrine
- **Reinforces, doesn't override:** delegation-first (token discipline), CEO operating doctrine, and the accountant still govern *execution*; the 8 Laws govern *integrity, non-attachment, and service* on top.
- **Generalises the firewall:** Law 7 (private = public) is the firewall doctrine stated as a universal principle.
- **The "8" resonance (note, don't mysticise):** Ashta = Sanskrit "eight"; McTaggart's *Power of Eight* (group intention) is a **different** concept from Schwartz's 8 Laws (change-maker traits) — both are consciousness-research lineage and both reinforce the "8" brand thread, but keep them distinct in copy.
