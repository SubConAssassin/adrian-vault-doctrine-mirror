# Protocol: Age-Regression Scanning

## §1 Origin + Lineage

The age-regression scanning protocol is the primary chronological diagnostic instrument of **Subconscious Surgery™**. It is designed to bypass the conscious mind's rationalisations and locate the precise developmental node where a trauma or limiting belief was originally anchored in the client's physiology:

* **Dr John Skype Mentorship:** In **Skype Session 5 (2014-11-21)**, Dr John trained Adrian in the clinical mechanics of locating inherited ancestral blockages and maternal stress projections anchored during the fetal or womb state.
* **Modality Codification:** Adrian expanded this training to establish the **Five Core Pivot Milestones** (Womb, Age 5, Age 10, Age 20, Age 30), creating a highly efficient scanning matrix that eliminates the high-friction, trial-and-error timeline searches used in traditional hypnotherapy.

## §2 Apparatus

1. **Participants:** The Practitioner (conducting the scan) and the Client (whose timeline is being scanned), or the surrogate proxy.
2. **Somatic Indicator:** The finger loop muscle test or stand-alone sway testing.
3. **The Chronological Matrix:** The structured list of developmental milestones:
   * **The Womb:** Inherited ancestral stress, pre-natal parental projections, and fetal nervous system programming.
   * **Age 5:** Early childhood identity, primary parental attachments, comfort-zone thresholds.
   * **Age 10:** Socialisation dynamics, peer judgment, school-based expression blocks.
   * **Age 20:** Independence conflicts, early romantic trauma, initial financial survival anxiety.
   * **Age 30:** Career alignment crises, deep-seated fear of failure, consolidation of the "Safe Route Pathology."

## §3 Method (step-by-step)

### Step 1: Establish Somatic Receptivity
1. Calibrate the client's autonomic baseline, verifying distinct strong (truth) and weak (falsehood) responses.
2. Ensure the client's prefrontal analytical mind is relaxed by performing a stomach meridian pattern interrupt if necessary.

### Step 2: Executing the Milestone Sweep
Rather than searching individual years at random, the practitioner queries the five major developmental nodes:
1. Query Womb: *"Did this emotional block anchor during the womb state?"* Apply muscle test. Observe response.
2. Query Age 5: *"Did this block anchor between birth and Age 5?"* Apply muscle test.
3. Query Age 10: *"Did this block anchor between Age 5 and Age 10?"* Apply muscle test.
4. Query Age 20: *"Did this block anchor between Age 10 and Age 20?"* Apply muscle test.
5. Query Age 30: *"Did this block anchor between Age 20 and Age 30?"* Apply muscle test.

### Step 3: Narrowing the Chronological Coordinate
Once a milestone query triggers a weak response (confirming the presence of a trauma block), the practitioner narrows the search to locate the exact year:
1. Example: If the scan flags the node between Age 5 and Age 10, the practitioner queries sequentially: *"Age 6?" "Age 7?" "Age 8?" "Age 9?"*
2. If the muscle test loop breaks at *Age 8*, the practitioner confirms: *"This block was anchored at Age 8."*

### Step 4: Isolating the Emotional Trigger
With the exact year identified, the practitioner queries the somatic database to identify the nature of the trauma:
1. Query key participants: *"Was this event linked to the mother? The father? A teacher? A sibling?"*
2. Query the core unhealed feeling: *"Was it fear of rejection? Abandonment? Lack of safety? Unfairness?"*
3. The practitioner verifies the match with a strong muscle test lock.

### Step 5: Integrating the Node into the Clearing Script
The identified age and trauma are woven directly into the customized cohesive statement (e.g. *"...which has been caused by long-term inappropriate unresolved feelings anchored at Age 8..."*), allowing the subsequent 6-repetition clearing loop to target and dissolve the specific childhood neural loop.

## §4 When Used

* **Phase 2 (Subconscious Clearing):** Executed immediately after identifying the somatic organ blockage, establishing the precise timeline context for the clearing execution.
* **Intake Discovery Sessions:** To give the client immediate logical proof of their subconscious programming, establishing credibility.

## §5 Common Pitfalls

1. **Analytical Guessing:** The practitioner or client attempts to guess the age based on conscious memory (e.g. *"I remember a bad thing happening at Age 12, so it must be that"*). The practitioner must trust the somatic feedback loop, even if the client has no conscious memory of the year flagged. The subconscious mind holds records that the conscious mind has repressed.
2. **Failing to Scan the Womb:** Many deep financial and relationship blocks are ancestral or maternal, anchored during gestation. Skipping the womb query leaves these foundational blocks untouched.
3. **Pacing Friction:** Rushing through the milestones without establishing a clean, zero-point baseline between queries leads to false readings. Take a breath between scans.
