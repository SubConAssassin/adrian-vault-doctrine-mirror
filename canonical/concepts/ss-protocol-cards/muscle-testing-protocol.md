# Protocol: Muscle Testing (Somatic Kinesiology Feedback)

## §1 Origin + Lineage

The muscle-testing protocol, or somatic kinesiology feedback loop, descends through a dual lineage. The conceptual basis was inherited from the Karol Truman oral clearing tradition, which associated physical somatic tension with specific unhealed emotional blockages. The diagnostic mechanical apparatus was formally introduced to Adrian Alan Taffinder by **Dr John DeVore** during their clinical Skype mentorship beginning in October 2014:

* **TTT Curriculum Integration:** In **Skype Session 1 (2014-10-24)**, Dr John trained Adrian in the physical mechanics of establishing a somatic baseline and calibrating binary (yes/no) indicators.
* **Modality Synthesis:** Adrian subsequently upgraded this diagnostic tool from a slow, high-friction, purely clinical inquiry into a rapid, highly scalable coaching instrument, introducing the **1–10 Quantifiable Reframe** to optimize corporate and personal decisions.

Verbatim grounding in the corpus:
> "testing your energy and its relationship with things at different points in time. You've got all sorts of things that you can test. Should I have a nap now? Will that actually give me a functional benefit in productivity?" 
*(Source: canonical/intelligence/mastermind-transcripts/mastermind-32-testing-synthesis.md)*

## §2 Apparatus

The setup requires a calm physical environment to eliminate analytical interference from the prefrontal cortex:

1. **Participants:** The Practitioner (facilitator) and the Client (operator), or the Primary Operator executing self-testing.
2. **Physical Setup:** The client must be seated comfortably, shoulders relaxed, breathing naturally to calibrate the autonomic nervous system.
3. **The Testing Instrument:** 
   * **Finger Loop Method:** The primary operator forms a circular ring using the thumb and index/middle finger of one hand, locking the tips securely. The other hand's index finger and thumb are inserted into the loop, attempting to push the loop open using a steady, controlled outward pull.
   * **Arm Resistance Method:** In 1:1 physical sessions, the client extends their dominant arm straight out at shoulder height. The practitioner applies gentle downward pressure on the wrist to test resistance.
   * **Whole-Body Sway Method:** The client stands straight with feet shoulder-width apart, arms hanging loosely. The practitioner monitors the body's natural forward (strong/truth) or backward (weak/falsehood) gravitational sway.

## §3 Method (step-by-step)

### Step 1: Autonomic Baseline Calibration
Before deep inquiries are made, the practitioner must verify the integrity of the autonomic circuit. The client is guided to declare absolute truths and absolute falsehoods to identify the somatic shift:
1. Command the client to state a verified fact: *"My name is [Real Name]."* Apply the test. The loop must remain strong and locked (rigid resistance).
2. Command the client to state a verified lie: *"My name is Steve."* (Assuming the name is not Steve). Apply the test. The loop must cleanly break open with minimal resistance.
3. If the circuit is "switched" (giving weak responses to truth or strong to lies), the client is instructed to drink water, massage the stomach meridian tap points under the eyes, and take three deep breaths to realign the electrical flow.

### Step 2: Formulating the Quantifiable Inquiry
The practitioner translates vague, subjective questions (e.g., *"Should I launch this book?"*) into highly specific, time-bound, 1–10 mathematical inquires:
* Banish the words *"should I"* or *"is it in my best interest"* — the subconscious cannot process subjective morality.
* Standard formula: *"On a score of 1 to 10, will executing [Action X] in the next 30 days result in a functional improvement in my financial capacity, where 5 is no change, 1 is total destruction, and 10 is exponential improvement?"*

### Step 3: Executing the Somatic Pulse
Apply steady, neutral force to the muscle testing loop. 
* A clean, unbreakable lock indicates a score of **6 or higher** (positive trajectory).
* An immediate break indicates a score of **4 or lower** (negative trajectory/blockage).
* Narrow the score by cascading: *"Is it a 6? Is it a 7? Is it an 8?"* until the loop breaks, establishing the exact somatic coordinate.

### Step 4: Removing Analytical Interference
If the client is over-analyzing or anxious about the outcome, the prefrontal cortex will override the autonomic nervous system, causing false positives. The practitioner commands a pattern interrupt:
1. Tap the stomach meridian points beneath the eyes.
2. Have the client repeat: *"I allow my mind to relax and be at peace."*
3. Re-test immediately while the client is exhaling.

## §4 When Used

* **Phase 1 (Discovery & Intake):** To scan and calibrate the client's baseline nervous system receptivity.
* **Phase 2 (Subconscious Clearing):** To identify the specific somatic organs holding blockages and narrow down the precise age of trauma during the regression scan.
* **Phase 4 (Mastermind Cohort / Integration):** To test the functional capacity of business opportunities, contract structures, or communication timing.

## §5 Common Pitfalls

1. **Greed and Egoic Bias:** If the operator is desperate for a specific outcome (e.g., wanting a business deal to test as a 10), their conscious desire will physically tense the fingers, manufacturing a false "strong" response. The operator must execute the **Bias Removal Protocol** prior to testing.
2. **Subjective Phrasing:** Asking vague questions like *"Should I move to Bali?"* yields erratic responses because "should" is an analytical judgment, not a somatic vector.
3. **Physical Dehydration:** A lack of hydration disrupts the body's electrical conductivity, causing weak or "switched" calibrations. Always have water present.
