## §2 Apparatus

1. **Participants:** The Practitioner (conducting the diagnostic scan) and the Client (whose somatic field is being mapped).
2. **Somatic Indicator:** Calibrated finger loop muscle testing or stand-alone sway testing.
3. **The Somatic Organ-Emotion Cartography Matrix:** The canonical database of body-organ to psychological-dynamic mappings:
   * **Heart:** Connection blocks, unhealed self-relationship, blocked capacity for self-trust, relationship grief.
   * **Brain / Head:** Worry, over-analytical resistance, analytical interference, safety-seeking through strategic overload.
   * **Liver:** Anger, unforgiveness of self/others, corporate resentment, active externalized blame, financial frustration.
   * **Throat:** Inability to speak truth, suppressed expression, survival-based silence.
   * **Transverse Colon:** Analytical resistance, lack of receptivity, closed boundaries, over-strategizing.
   * **Stomach Meridian:** Autonomic startle response, baseline security breach, primary fear origin.
   * **Knees:** Inflexibility, stubbornness, resisting career pivots or life transitions.
   * **Hemorrhoids:** Boundary failures, carrying irritation caused by a close personal or professional partner.

## §3 Method (step-by-step)

### Step 1: Client Autonomic Alignment
1. Calibrate the client's nervous system to ensure a clean, unswitched baseline (strong to truth, weak to falsehood).
2. instruct the client to close their eyes, direct their attention inward, and breathe naturally.

### Step 2: Biological Field Scanning
The practitioner scans the client's physical coordinates chronologically or via energetic pull:
1. Query the head/brain: *"Is there an emotional block in the head/brain?"* Apply muscle test. (A weak break confirms a block of analytical overload).
2. Query the heart: *"Is there a block in the heart chakra?"* Apply muscle test. (A weak break confirms relationship or self-trust blockages).
3. Query the liver: *"Is there a block in the liver meridian?"* Apply muscle test. (A weak break confirms resentment/blame blockages).
4. Query the throat: *"Is there a block in the throat meridian?"* Apply muscle test. (A weak break confirms expression/truth blockages).
5. Query the transverse colon: *"Is there an analytical block in the transverse colon?"* Apply muscle test. (A weak break confirms receptivity blockages).

### Step 3: Mapping the Emotion
With the biological coordinate identified, the practitioner queries the emotional database:
1. Example: If the liver meridian flags weak, the practitioner scans: *"Is this unhealed feeling anger? Is it unforgiveness? Is it resentment? Is it active blame?"*
2. Confirm the match with a strong muscle test lock: *"It is active blame."*

### Step 4: Quantifying the Severity
The practitioner translates the block into a quantifiable metric:
1. Ask the client's nervous system: *"On a scale of 1 to 10, what is the functional capacity of this organ's energy, where 10 is absolute free flow and 1 is complete block?"*
2. Sequentially count: *"Is it a 5? Is it a 4? Is it a 3?"* The loop breaks at the active coordinate, establishing the severity.

### Step 5: Documenting the Diagnostic Coordinate
Record the coordinate (e.g. *Liver | Resentment | Severity: 3/10 | Age: 12*) and weave these precise coordinates directly into the custom cohesive statement.

## §4 When Used

## §5 Common Pitfalls

1. **Medical Diagnostic Overreach:** The practitioner must **NEVER** frame organ-emotion mapping as physical medical diagnostics. Physical disease must never be diagnosed or claimed to be cured. The mapping is utilized strictly as an emotional metaphor and somatic feedback indicator.
2. **Confusing Symptoms with Blocks:** Scanning for the physical symptom (e.g. *a sinus headache*) rather than the underlying emotional block (e.g. *wanting to control someone else's life*) results in superficial clearings. Always seek the unhealed feeling.
3. **Leading the Circuit:** The practitioner tenses their fingers unconsciously during the diagnostic scan, forcing a "strong" response because they expect a block to be there. Bias removal must be executed prior to every scan.
